# Growth model

Last reviewed: 2026-07-19

## North-star outcome

Help the right parent, school, or learner take the next confident step toward a MakerLab program, mission, booking, or Store product — then make the result visible enough to create the next step and referral.

## Connected journey

| Stage | Visitor need | Primary surfaces | Desired action |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Discover | “What is MakerLab and can I trust it?” | SEO pages, blog, social landing pages, Home | Read a relevant answer or program page |
| Understand | “What will my child actually build?” | Program, mission, Store, Maker Wall | View outcomes and tools |
| Choose | “Where should we start?” | Quiz, recommended mission, program comparison | Accept one recommended next step |
| Convert | “Can I book without risk?” | Booking, free workshop/trial, Store | Submit lead, reserve, or buy |
| Prove | “Was it worthwhile?” | Project proof, certificate, portfolio, follow-up | Share result / continue |
| Progress | “What comes next?” | Program detail, parent follow-up, next mission | Take the next offer |

## Required page logic

Every meaningful public page must make clear:

1. who it is for;
2. what real outcome is built;
3. why it matters now;
4. the credible proof or risk reducer;
5. one recommended next action; and
6. the next step after that action.

## Measurement backlog

Do not add tracking blindly. First agree the analytics provider, consent approach, responsible owner, and reporting cadence. Once authorized, measure at least:

- `program_view`, `mission_view`, `product_view`, `blog_view`;
- `quiz_start`, `quiz_complete`, `recommended_offer_view`;
- `booking_start`, `booking_submit`, `trial_booking_submit`;
- `store_checkout_start`, `store_purchase` when checkout is connected;
- qualified lead source, landing page, and selected offer.

## SEO and LLM content principles

- Build pages around real parent/school questions, local intent, clear structured headings, evidence, and useful internal links.
- Give each content cluster one intent, one primary landing page, a proof source, and a conversion link.
- Maintain technical basics: title, description, canonical/robots as appropriate, accessible semantics, fast images, and crawlable internal links.
- Treat LLM visibility as a result of clear factual content and brand/entity consistency, not a separate magic trick.
